Data presented comes from public and institutional sources. Ranges reflect variability by country, project size and local conditions. Values do not constitute investment recommendations. FAQ

What is solar PPA?
A PPA (Power Purchase Agreement) is a long-term energy purchase contract between a solar producer and a buyer (company, public entity). The price is fixed for 10-25 years.
Which countries have the lowest solar PPA in Europe?
Spain and Portugal show the lowest values in Europe thanks to high irradiation, competitive labor costs and a mature market.
How is solar PPA evolving in Europe?
The trend is generally downward thanks to economies of scale, technological improvement and increased competition. The decline is more pronounced in the utility-scale segment.
What are the data sources?
Data comes from LevelTen Energy PPA Price Index Q4 2025, Pexapark PPA Tracker 2025, AFRY (ex-Pöyry) Market Reports, BloombergNEF (public summaries). All sources are public and verifiable.
How often is the data updated?
Data is updated annually (Q1) from institutional source publications.
